Election Period

#446

An election has an effective date range that govern both its coverage period and cost period.

The coverage begins on the start date of the election and ends on the end date of the election. The actual coverage may be suspended due to a coverage gap period.

The cost for an election is monthly and is not prorated. The election that determines the cost for a given month is the election that includes the 1st of the given month.

General Design Considerations and Questions - DRB

#399

Qualifying Events and Election Approvals

#411

In general DRB now considers almost all employment changes as a qualifying event. BERT is currently only receiving employment changes when one of the three following elements change:

- Bargaining Unit
- Part/Full Time Indicator
- Health Eligibility Indicator

In addition BERT also receives the following employment events that are considered qualifying events:

- Hire/Rehire
- Return from LWOP/Layoff

Elections for other qualifying events will be subject to DRB approval since there is no information to match against.

Elections for the events BERT knows about will be automatically approved once they come in from IRIS. They will be matched on the following criteria:

- Bargaining Unit - exact match
- Full/Part Time Indicator - exact match
- Health Eligibility Indicator - must always be Y
- Event Date - within the system set range (e.g. +/- 5 days)

If the election Event Date is off by more than the system set range, then DRB staff approval is required. If the BargainUnit or Full/Part Time Indicator does not match, it will be ignored/rejected.

Election Overrides

HINS Z Code Carryover between Service

#413

1. BERT will respond to HINS “Z” codes more broadly than SIMS does today.  In particular, BERT will not provide coverage to an otherwise-health-eligible member if Finance has a Z code in place.  A Z code with an effective date on or before a member’s hire date will prevent them from enrolling in benefits.  Additionally, a Z code that arises during the course of a member’s employment will result in coverage being cancelled from that point forward.  (Note that SIMS currently implements this second “after-arising” rule and ignores Z codes on or before the hire date.)  The rules of when coverage will be stopped/started in response to changes in the Z status (e.g., F -> Z or Z -> F) will be as follows:
1. When Finance turns “on” the Z code, coverage will end based on the first 1st rule – if the effective date of the Z code is the 1st of a month, then coverage ends on the last day of the prior month; otherwise, coverage will end on the last day of the month of the Z code effective date (Examples: Z code effective 3/1/2024 => coverage end date 2/29/2024.  Z code effective 3/2/2024 => coverage end date 3/31/2024.)
2. When Finance turns “off” the Z code, coverage will start on the effective date of the new health-eligible code (i.e., on the effective date of the “F” or “P” that follows the “Z”).

LWOP End Date Interpretation

#253

For historical reasons, SIMS currently defines LWOP_END_DATE as the first day back at work (i.e., the first day *not* on LWOP).

Discussed in meeting with Health team on 4/11/2022 -- decided to change this going forward in BERT to align with business understanding of representing instead the last day on LWOP.

Note that this change also matches the interpretation of Service events (stand and end dates are *inclusive* -- i.e., the Termination Date/End Date for Service is the last day at work and not the first day not at work). This also align with how the LWOP segment appears in IRIS -- there is a Start Date and End Date and then the next record shows the "Return to Work" event starting on the day following the LWOP End Date.

The new interface view from Employer Services (EMPSRV.BERT_EMPLOYMENT_EVENT, which is represented in BERT by the synonym ES_EMPLOYMENT_EVENT) will be written to reflect this, and so the END_DATE field for the core BERT table EMPLOYMENT_EVENT should also be interpreted to be the last day of the period for ALL Employment Event Types.

Period Start/End Dates

#213

All periods that have a start and end date, will be inclusive.

The start date is the first effective date of the period.
The end date is the last effective date of the period.

For example a LWOP start date is the first day that the employee is NOT at work and the end date is the last date that the employee is NOT at work.

Business Day Definition

#248

For purposes of rules involving cost and coverage and the first of the month, most definitions specify the first business day of the month.

The first business day of the month is normally the first day of the month unless:

- The first day is a weekend day (Saturday or Sunday) in which case the first business day will be Monday.
- The first day of the month is a Holiday in which case the first business day will be the next non-weekend day.

In event of LWOP, coverage begins on 31st consecutive day that employee is in pay status from end date of LWOP.

Term/Rehire

If you are are rehired within 7 days from end date of coverage, your coverage resumes the day you return to work. If not, you follow the New Employee process.

Notes from 8/6 Requirements meeting:

Term/Rehire

- Generally, a termination followed by a rehire triggers the 31-day waiting period again
- _Exception_: if a member is terminated and then rehired in benefits-eligible position 7 calendar days or fewer from the end of coverage in prior position, then coverage resumes immediately upon rehire (no waiting period)
- Example 1: Member has health coverage, terminates on 8/7/2020. Member is rehired on 9/7/2020. Coverage ended on 8/31/2020, so the rehire is within the 7-day window and coverage starts immediately on 9/7/2020.
- Example 2: Same facts as Example 1, except that the rehire date is 9/8/2020 instead. Now, member is outside the 7-day window, so must go through waiting period again. Coverage in new position will start on 10/08/2020 (assuming member is continually in pay status during waiting period).
- This only applies to actual service terminations / separation events. For example, it does not apply to Layoff, which operates the same as a normal LWOP/LOA event

Other Transaction Types

#148

Other Transaction Types
---
The following is a list of the other transaction types.

- Employee/Employer Direct Payments - these are either payments to the State by the employee (usually by a check) or payments made via a transfer of funds in the State accounting system in a transaction outside payroll. This is a credit on the account.
- Employee/Employer Direct Refunds - these are either refunds of credit balance made to the employee from the State accounting system or refunds made via a transfer of funds in the State accounting system in a transaction outside payroll. This is a charge on the account.
- Write-Off - this transaction simply writes-off an open amount for the employee or employer. These can be either positive or negative amounts. This is a credit on the account if positive, otherwise a charge on the account.
